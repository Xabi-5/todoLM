Toponimy of Rome

Javier Lodeiro

19/10/2022

According to the legend of the foundation of Rome, the name of the city, Roma would come from that of Romulus, the founder and first king. Although, it has also been suggested that it could be the other way round, Romulus could come from Roma. The most popular hypotheses currently are:

  • From Rumon or Rumen, believed to be the archaic name of the Tiber river, and related to greek and latin words terms for water flow
  • From Etruscan Ruma, word for teat, referring to the wolf that breastfed Romulus and Remus
However, the roots of the origin of the name remain uncertain to this day
